About Dr. Hani Zahran

I’m Dr. Hani Zahran, Vice Chairman of the Saudi Society for Geoscience and Manager of the Disaster Center at the Saudi Geological Survey. With over 24 years in the geoscience field, I focus on managing and studying natural hazards such as earthquakes and volcanoes. I am a seasoned Geophysicist and Seismologist with extensive experience across governmental and research sectors. As a Consultant for Earthquakes and Volcanoes at the Saudi Geological Survey since April 2019, and previously as General Manager of the National Center for Earthquake and Volcanoes from 2005 to 2019, I have led significant projects on national safety and seismic research.

My career began at the French Geological Survey (BRGM), where I served from 1993 to 2001, progressing from Geophysicist to Senior Geophysicist. Returning to Saudi Arabia, I managed the Geophysical Exploration Department and later the Geophysical Management at the Saudi Geological Survey, focusing on seismic data analysis and monitoring of seismic activities.

My expertise includes seismicity monitoring, seismic signal processing, and utilizing geophysical methods and numerical simulations for earthquake and volcano research. I have presented our findings at international workshops and contributed to peer-reviewed publications. I am also committed to teaching, training, and consulting, enhancing the skills of future geophysicists and seismologists.

With a strong commitment to continuous professional development, I am adept at working in multicultural environments, marked by a rigorous analytical approach, critical thinking, and a positive, detail-oriented attitude.
